At my previous company, I was working on a cross-functional project with a team of people who didn't report to me. One of the engineers on the team was consistently late with their deliverables, and it was starting to cause issues for the rest of us.

Since I wasn't their manager, I decided to talk to them directly. I set up a meeting and explained the situation. I told them that the delays were affecting the team's ability to stay on schedule and that I wanted to understand what was going on. They said they had a lot on their plate and weren't sure how to prioritize.

We talked about it and came up with a plan for them to communicate earlier when things were slipping. After that conversation, things improved and the project got back on track.

I think the main takeaway is that sometimes you just need to have a direct conversation with people even when you don't have authority over them. People are usually pretty reasonable when you approach them respectfully.
