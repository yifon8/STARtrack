At my previous company, I was working on a cross-functional project — some kind of product launch, I think, though I'm a bit fuzzy on the exact context. One of the engineers on the team kept missing their deliverables — I believe it was happening across a few milestones, maybe two or three occasions. It was causing delays for the rest of us.

Since I wasn't their manager, I decided to try addressing it directly. I put together a rough visual of some kind showing how the delays were affecting the timeline — I'm not sure it was the clearest thing, but I thought it was worth trying.

I asked if we could have a quick conversation about it. When I brought it up, they mentioned having competing demands from elsewhere, though they didn't get very specific. I wasn't entirely sure how to respond to that.

We talked it through and I suggested they try to flag things earlier when something was slipping. I think they were receptive — or at least I got that impression. After that, their delivery did seem to improve and the project finished on time.

I think approaching it directly was probably the right move, even if I'm not sure I always found the right words for it.
